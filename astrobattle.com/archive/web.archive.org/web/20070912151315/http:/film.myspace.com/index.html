    In Theaters
    Sean Penn has made a name for himself as a director with a series of edgy features. With Into the Wild, Penn has graduated even further. Based on the wildly successful bestseller by John Krakauer, this true story follows young Christopher McCandless, a discontent college-grad who donates his entire savings to charity and resolves to hike to Alaska in pursuit of adventure. Star-in-the-making Emile Hirsh, who delivers a tour-de-force performance, plays McCandless. Penn spent an obsessive eight months sharpening this film from five hours to two and a half, and the final product is a testament to his unwavering dedication.


     

    Filmmaker Interview
    In 2005 indie director Larry Fessenden was troubled by the state of the world—specifically, by our leaders’ callow response to the threat of global warming. So he did what he does best: He made a horror movie. In The Last Winter Fessenden combines John Carpenter's The Thing and An Inconvenient Truth to create a creepy horror film that's also socially conscience. Damom Smith discusses the film with Fessenden for Filmmaker.
